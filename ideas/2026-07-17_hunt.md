## 1. Key literature from the frozen archive

These papers were selected through targeted prior-work search for I3: the hypothesis that control transfer from video-pretrained world models derives from a passive appearance prior rather than action-conditioned dynamics.

- **A2World** — Learning Transferable Dynamics Priors from Action to World Modeling | https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29501 | Pretrains an action-conditioned diffusion world model on large action-labeled robot data, then transfers it to simulation and policy learning. It is the closest counterexample: it compares action-conditioned pretraining against generic or text-video initialization on LIBERO, reaches the opposite conclusion that action conditioning carries the transfer, lacks a shuffle arm, and reports only about 1.6% between text pretraining at 97.4% and action-video pretraining at 98.6% in LIBERO Table 7. That value falls within the idea's “≤3% means non-load-bearing” band. **Strongest counterexample.**
- **What Makes Video World Model Latents Action-Relevant: Prediction over Reconstruction** | https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07687 | Uses inverse-dynamics probing on CALVIN to attribute action relevance to temporal prediction rather than reconstruction fidelity. It is also an attribution study, but its axis is prediction versus reconstruction, it credits action-aware or inverse-dynamics supervision, and it does not run the equal-budget shuffled-action transfer comparison.
- **GR-1** — Unleashing Large-Scale Video Generative Pre-training | https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.13139 | Uses action-free web-video prediction pretraining followed by robot fine-tuning. Its ablation compares video pretraining with no video pretraining; it has neither an action-conditioned arm nor a shuffle control.
- **GR-2** — Generative Video-Language-Action Model | https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.06158 | Pretrains on 38 million action-free internet videos before action fine-tuning, without an equal-budget shuffle or action-removal comparison.
- **LAPO** — Learning to Act without Actions | https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.10812 | Infers latent actions and a latent-action policy from action-free video. It establishes that real action labels are not required for useful policy pretraining, but still learns action structure and does not claim a passive appearance prior.
- **AMPLIFY** — Actionless Motion Priors for Robot Learning from Videos | https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.14198 | Trains forward dynamics on action-free video and inverse dynamics on a small action-labeled set. It shows that action-free video supplies much of the dynamics prior, but does not include an equal-budget correct-versus-shuffled-action control.
- **WALA** — Learning Executable Latent Actions from Action-Labeled Demonstrations and Action-Free Videos | https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11397 | Assigns executable-control supervision to action-labeled demonstrations and dynamics supervision to action-free video. The sources are complementary, and the paper does not isolate whether action conditioning is load-bearing.
- **iVideoGPT** — Interactive VideoGPTs are Scalable World Models | https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.15223 | Studies an action-conditioned video-prediction world model with component ablations and mixed-pretraining transfer. Its component ablations are not shuffle controls and do not attribute gains to a passive prior.

## 2. Frozen qualifying-idea record

### I3 · Action conditioning is not load-bearing for control transfer from video-pretrained world models

> **All 3 independent frozen-archive verdicts were Strong Accept. The official ledger verdict is now accept-w-rev.**

**Archived idea**

One-sentence story: Video-pretrained world models transfer to control because they learn a passive prior over what the future looks like, rather than because they learn action-conditioned dynamics. Action conditioning is not load-bearing during pretraining.

Theme: world-model training objective

Form: bottleneck-diagnosis experiment with an action-free web-video data arm

Summary: The prevailing account assigns the value of video world-model pretraining to action-conditioned dynamics. The competing explanation is that pretraining mainly supplies a passive prior over scene and object evolution, while the action-conditioning signal is weak and cheap to rebuild during downstream fine-tuning. Shuffling or removing pretraining action labels should therefore preserve nearly all downstream control transfer. If supported, the model could be pretrained directly on abundant action-free web video rather than scarce action-labeled robot data. This claim concerns the source of pretraining transfer, not action-decay-induced drift within a rollout.

Minimal falsification experiment: Train a small latent video world model in simulation, with correctly action-conditioned pretraining as the strongest baseline in the style of GR-1/Genie. Compare three arms—correct actions, shuffled actions, and no actions—under equal downstream fine-tuning, then measure LIBERO control gain with ≥3 seeds per arm. If action conditioning is load-bearing, the shuffled arm should collapse. If the passive prior dominates, all arms should remain close. A shuffled-minus-correct downstream success-rate gap of ≤3% supports “not load-bearing”; >10% falsifies the idea. The small-model experiment fits on one GPU.

Why it appeared novel: Video world-model transfer papers typically treat action conditioning as a central selling point. Equal-budget shuffled-action controls that isolate the passive-prior contribution were rare and required verification.

Top three archived actions:

1. Restrict the headline to the falsifiable negative claim that action-conditioned pretraining is not load-bearing; retain the passive appearance prior as an explanation to test rather than an established attribution.
2. Add the shuffled-action arm directly to A2World's LIBERO setup and reinterpret its reported 1.6% under the same experimental conditions.
3. Add an intermediate arm with partially corrupted action labels to map the load-bearing curve and resolve the 3–10% ambiguity band.

Claim-occupancy audit:

- Published competing explanation: none as a headline claim. The literature argued in the opposite direction. A2World (2606.29501) explicitly attributed improved transfer over generic-video initialization to stronger action-to-dynamics priors; 2606.07687 attributed action relevance to action-aware or inverse-dynamics supervision. Many action-free video-pretraining systems—GR-1/2, UniPi, LAPO/LAPA, AMPLIFY, PreLAR, Vid2World, and DINO-WM “Back to the Features”—treated action-free data as a response to annotation cost while still recovering or learning action structure. None made the causal claim that action conditioning is a non-load-bearing bottleneck and transfer derives from a passive future-appearance prior.
- Named estimand or problem definition: none. The equal-budget three-arm action-shuffle design for isolating the passive-prior contribution had not been named. Action shuffling appeared elsewhere for other purposes: Hallucination in World Models (2606.27326) used an “action shuffle ratio” as an internal controllability or sensitivity metric; Privileged Foresight Distillation (2604.25859) used a “shuffled-future control.” Neither isolated equal-budget pretraining transfer.
- Target-paper self-assessment from limitations, ablations, and discussions:
  - GR-1/GR-2: pretraining itself uses action-free video prediction; the ablation compares video pretraining with no video pretraining and has neither an action-conditioned arm nor a shuffle arm, so it does not test whether action conditioning is load-bearing.
  - Genie: latent actions are learned without supervision; ablations cover the tokenizer, video fidelity, and controllability rather than a shuffle control for downstream control transfer.
  - UniPi: video-as-policy plus an IDM extracts actions; its ablations cover components and seen instructions, not shuffled-action pretraining.
  - iVideoGPT: action-conditioned world model with component ablations and mixed-pretraining transfer; no shuffle control or passive-prior attribution.
  - VPT: the method restores actions with an IDM and then applies behavioral cloning, making action labels central; it provides no admission that action conditioning is unnecessary.
  - LAPA: acknowledges that latent actions can encode camera or appearance motion and argues that real action labels are unnecessary, but does not claim that action conditioning is not load-bearing for transfer.
  - A2World (2606.29501): the closest paper. LIBERO Table 7 reports text Cosmos initialization at 97.0%, text pretraining at 97.4%, action-video pretraining at 98.6%, and policy-targeted pretraining at 98.8%. It concludes that action-conditioned transfer is better and has no shuffled-action negative control. Its reported difference of about 1.6% lies within the idea's “≤3% means non-load-bearing” band.

Strongest counterexample: A2World (Learning Transferable Dynamics Priors from Action to World Modeling, 2606.29501). It compares action-conditioned pretraining with generic or text-video initialization on LIBERO, covering the correct-action and no-action arms of the proposed three-arm design, but (1) reaches the opposite conclusion, (2) lacks the critical shuffled-action arm, and (3) reports a gap of only about 1.6%, within the proposal's non-load-bearing band. The proposed headline depended on using the shuffle arm to attribute that 1.6% to a weak signal cheap to reconstruct; A2World neither ran nor made that interpretation.

Overlap: low. No single paper covered the headline—action-conditioned pretraining is not load-bearing and transfer comes from a passive future-appearance prior—using an equal-budget correct/shuffled/no-action LIBERO comparison. The nearest papers either reached the opposite conclusion, including A2World and 2606.07687, or used action-free video as a cost workaround without the isolation experiment, including GR-1/2, LAPO/LAPA, and AMPLIFY. Search boundaries: the mainstream position favored action conditioning; the S2 API result was unavailable; action-free transfer itself was already mature, so the overlap was low rather than empty.

Papers read: 10 at abstract plus method or ablation depth—2606.29501, 2606.07687, 2607.11397, 2607.09185, 2511.02776, 2607.13017, 2607.06558, 2607.10203, 2606.27325, and 2604.16391. GR-1/GR-2/Genie/VPT/UniPi/iVideoGPT/AMPLIFY/LAPO/LAPA were covered at abstract plus ablation-summary depth.
