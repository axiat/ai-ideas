# S1 Experiment Record Through 2026-07-20

Setup: frozen π0.5 flow VLA + DSRL, with SAC operating in a 32-dimensional flow-noise space + LIBERO, where this fork's `libero_10` contains an expanded set of 2519 task variants. Every closed-loop run uses sparse success reward, 500k steps × 3 seeds, and an H100 80G.

## 1. Question and claim

**Question:** Can the field's open-loop evidence standards—capacity, separability, and probe accuracy in the style of PIVOT/MOKA/SoM/R3M—predict closed-loop RL utility when interpretable channels such as visual arrow overlays, keypoints, and text become control surfaces for a frozen VLA?

**Motivation:** Three established observations define the problem. (i) DSRL shows that a frozen flow VLA can improve under RL using noise alone: SAC learns a policy in the 32-dimensional initial-noise space without changing model weights. (ii) Human-readable ports beyond noise, including arrows drawn on the image and edits to instruction text, already control large models in PIVOT/MOKA/SoM and are justified through open-loop decodability. (iii) NLP and LLM work on probing, ITI, and RepE distinguishes information that can be read from information that can be written back. Connecting the ports in (ii) to the RL framework in (i) exposes the untested extrapolation from open-loop evidence to RL usability.

**Current claim:** A read–write gap separates open-loop decodability from closed-loop steerability. Open-loop metrics have no predictive value for RL utility and can be anti-predictive. Control authority—the action displacement relative to the task's behavioral gap—is necessary. Its magnitude is not sufficient: the **structure of writable directions** must spread authority across directions SAC can exploit. Concentrating high authority in a few hypersensitive directions is actively harmful.

## 2. Metrics and arms

| Concept | Operational definition |
|---|---|
| read, or open-loop decodability | capacity, a Gaussian MI proxy; directional separability sep=−cos, which is scale-invariant and therefore blind to magnitude |
| write, or closed-loop steerability | authority = the geometry probe's jump, the maximum ‖Δaction‖ between adjacent control-grid cells, measured with inference alone in minutes; cross-task ratio = jump / same-task noise_2d jump |
| methodological gate | valid step0 base: untrained SAC + fresh-per-replan noise ≤0.3, excluding the free-retry ceiling; rule 2: a same-task noise_2d collapse creates a “2-dimensional control surface is insufficient” confound and invalidates the point; every key prediction is preregistered before execution |

### Arms and injection mechanisms (`train_utils_sim.py:569-605`)

- **noise:** RL outputs 32 dimensions and fully replaces the initial flow noise. This is the original DSRL arm and upper-bound reference.
- **noise_2d:** RL outputs 2 dimensions. Each replan still samples fresh 32-dimensional random noise and adds the offset `(fixed random orthogonal 32×2 basis @ z)·√32`. RL can move the full noise cloud only along a fixed 2-dimensional plane, while the other 30 directions remain random. Degrees of freedom and background randomness match overlay/text; only the port differs, giving a dimension-matched control.
- **overlay:** Interpret 2 dimensions as direction (dx,dy), draw an arrow on the camera image, and feed the result to the model as a PIVOT-style visual prompt.
- **text:** Discretize 2 dimensions into direction words such as “ move left” and append them to the instruction. π0.5 ignores the input, making this dead-channel negative control the chance floor.
- **noise_2d_lowauth:** Use the noise_2d injection with a manually selected basis of low- or high-sensitivity directions, making authority an intervention variable.

### Training-free measurement procedure

Fix a scene, set the control level, and run inference ~24 times per level to record a level-to-action-samples table. The three metrics answer distinct questions. Capacity and sep are implemented in `sanity_channel_capacity.py`; jump is implemented in `control_surface_geometry.py`.

- **capacity:** The Gaussian MI proxy asks whether the control level can be inferred from the action, in bits. Any stable distributional difference scores, independent of displacement magnitude.
- **sep = −cos(Δleft, Δright):** Tests whether mean action displacements for opposing intents point in opposite directions. Cosine normalization removes magnitude.
- **jump, from the geometry probe:** On a 5×5 control grid, measure the maximum mean absolute action displacement ‖Δa‖ between adjacent cells. This is the only magnitude-sensitive metric. The probe also reports SNR above the noise floor and R²lin for surface smoothness.

## 4. Core results

### 4.1 Reversal: the best open-loop channel was the worst closed-loop surface (four arms on t838)

| Arm | AUC / final SR | Open-loop metric |
|---|---|---|
| noise 32d | 0.797 / 0.97 | — |
| noise_2d | 0.626 / 0.93 | sep is undefined for random directions |
| text, dead channel | 0.342 / 0.33 | C=0.00b |
| **overlay** | **0.206 / 0.30** | **C=1.88b, sep=+1.00, highest overall** |

The channel with the highest capacity and perfect directional separability was the worst control surface, below the dead channel. A healthy dimension-matched noise_2d arm excludes task difficulty and dimensionality as explanations.

### 4.2 Mechanism: authority, not surface roughness

Geometry probe values, reported as SNR / R2lin / jump: overlay 6.4/0.52/**0.18**, noise_2d 19/0.49/**1.98**, and text 0.11/0.58/0.04. R²≈0.5 for all three surfaces falsifies the “overlay is too rough to optimize” account. Overlay is detectable, with SNR>1, but cannot move the policy; its jump is 11× lower. In the strength sweep from 0.5→2, jump increased only from 0.12→0.29. Extrapolation requires strength≈13, where the arrow fills the image, while R2lin deteriorates above 1 because of occlusion distortion. The port cannot gain authority structurally.

### 4.3 Decoupling: sep ⊥ authority across 8 tasks and 16 rows

Pearson +0.25 was driven by one point; Spearman +0.07 showed no rank relationship. Equal sep corresponded to a 60× authority range: t276 arrow +0.98→ratio1.0 versus t758 kp +0.85→0.016. Negative sep retained authority at t1958 −0.04→0.54 and t1170 −0.24→closed-loop 0.986. Open-loop separability cannot rank candidate control surfaces.

### 4.4 Attribution: authority rather than nativeness in the t838 within-task 2×2

| | High authority | Low authority |
|---|---|---|
| Native | noise_2d 0.627/**0.940** | lowauth 0.333/0.387 |
| External | overlay@276 0.998, later invalidated by the step0 audit | overlay 0.206/0.253 |

Holding nativeness fixed and removing authority collapses performance to the overlay/text floor, identifying authority as the driver. Within the native 32-dimensional space, authority varies by two orders of magnitude: 1.985 versus 0.013.

### 4.5 Dose response: direction structure dominates authority magnitude (completed 07-20; both preregistered predictions falsified)

The basis-construction probe found a rank-~2 J^TJ spectrum on all four tasks: top-2 eigenvalues were O(1), while the rest were O(1e-5). A random 2-dimensional basis therefore already reached near-maximal authority at jump~2.0, while mid≈low.

Mean late-50% SR across 3 seeds:

| Task | noise 32d | native-low (j~.01) | mid | **native-high (j~2.0)** | **random-2d (j~2.0)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 838 | ✓ | 0.375 | 0.388 | **0.000** | **0.94** |
| 876 | 0.68 | 0.065 | 0.055 | 0.000 | 0.10 |
| 555 | 0.951 | 0.206 | 0.251 | 0.260 (s0 only) | 0.275 (s0 only) |

- Prediction ①, “engineered native-high reproduces random,” was **falsified in the opposite direction**. On 838 at the same jump2.0, random learned to 0.94 while the top 2 eigenvectors produced 0.00 across all three seed. Equal authority produced opposite outcomes. **Direction structure** is the deciding variable: displacement concentrated in 2 hypersensitive directions creates bang-bang control that cannot make fine adjustments, while displacement spread across many directions remains controllable.
- Prediction ②, “within-task jump is monotonic,” was **falsified**. On 838, the near-zero settings at 0.375/0.388 exceeded high at 0.000. The near-zero result is a free-retry floor rather than learning; strong high-direction perturbations actively move the policy away from the task and fall below the floor.
- Noise passed the validity gate on all three tasks, producing a clean within-2-dimensional comparison. The same-task, same-jump high-versus-random contrast on 838, 0.00 versus 0.94, directly supports the direction-structure claim.

## 5. Falsification and invalidation history

1. t237 invalid task, 06-22: the negative control did not die, exposing the fresh-noise free-retry confound.
2. t838 primary hypothesis falsified, 06-23: the overlay-capacity account failed and the supported reversal redirected the analysis to authority.
3. step0 audit, 07-10: the successful side of the 5-task monotonic result was invalidated as a ceiling artifact, establishing the valid step0 base gate.
4. Two preregistered scans, 07-08 / 07-15: both were inconclusive. The cross-task ratio prediction was structurally untestable because 0 tasks among 8 draws from a 130-task low-base pool simultaneously provided headroom, 2-dimensional controllability, and no ceiling.
5. Dose response, 07-20: both predictions were falsified, replacing a monotonic-authority law with the stronger direction-structure result.

Every prediction was locked before execution in `s1_prediction_preregistration.md` and scored literally.

## 6. Claim status

**Supported:**

1. Open-loop decodability standards fail for frozen-policy RL control-surface selection and can reverse, supported by the four-arm 838 result and the 8-task sep⊥authority decoupling.
2. Authority is necessary: the 838 2×2 collapses when authority is removed, independent of nativeness.
3. Authority is insufficient, and concentrated high authority is actively harmful. At equal jump, distributed random directions outperform concentrated top eigenvectors by 0.94 versus 0.00 on 838.
4. The noise-to-action sensitivity spectrum of frozen π0.5 is rank-~2 on all four measured tasks.
5. Methodological findings: enforce the valid step0 base gate; run the noise_2d control to 500k with multiple seed; do not extrapolate a 150k feasibility signal.

**Falsified or invalidated:** Cross-task monotonic authority on the successful side; within-task jump monotonicity; equivalence between engineered top eigenvectors and random directions; closed-loop meaning in the Gate-1 capacity ranking; and the “rough overlay surface” account.

**Boundary:** One policy, π0.5, and one simulator, LIBERO. The clean positive case is concentrated on 838. Task 555 has large seed-dominated variance. Evaluation uses 10 episodes at 0.1 resolution. The “distributed directions enable control” mechanism combines the within-task contrast on 838 with consistent collapse across three tasks; it is not a cross-task scan.

## 7. Paper framing (`s1_paper_framing.md`)

- Title: **Decodable Is Not Steerable**—open-loop capacity and separability do not predict the utility of an RL control surface for a frozen VLA.
- Introduction: connect the NLP probing critique and the read–write gap in LLM steering to the first quantitative embodied closed-loop instance.
- Evidence-bounded claims: (i) open-loop metrics fail and can be anti-predictive; (ii) authority is necessary through the 2×2, while direction structure explains sufficiency through the same-jump contrast; (iii) the geometry probe's jump is an inference-only estimator.
- Related work: evaluate the extrapolation from PIVOT/MOKA/SoM/R3M evidence standards to RL control-surface selection, not those original systems; place authority against the Gramian and empowerment-capacity line without claiming its invention; connect the read–write gap to ITI/RepE/V-info.
