## Conclusion: Keep the Clauses; the Gold Set Materials Were the Bug

Both questioned clauses produced the correct result after the honest-material rerun:

1. **Mechanism transfer requires a nontrivial adaptation**: the reasoning that held meanflow to AwR was correct. The honest material has high overlap and should reject. The clause was not too strict; the earlier material's false low-overlap report elevated a negative to AwR. **No change**.
2. **Diagnostic ceiling**: this is conditional—it applies only when none of the five dimensions scores 8+—rather than an absolute prohibition on benchmarks. The three robomme seats cited a combined ceiling: novelty was occupied by the taxonomy, constructing a credible benchmark at scale carried a single-researcher feasibility MAJOR, and no dimension scored 8+. The diagnostic ceiling was not the sole blocker. A genuinely low-overlap benchmark with high Broader value would score 8+ and escape the ceiling. robomme did not reach 8+ because its honest overlap was medium, so the ceiling was correct. **No change**.

After the web and industry-placeholder "no hits" records were added, the axiom-deletion channel's pos-axiom-adam case received 3/3 SA. Every vote named all four conditions. This proves the channel mechanism was correct and that the preceding 1/3 result came entirely from material below the threshold.

The actual defect was the gold set: priorwork for two of the three "positives" falsely reported low overlap and hid real occupants. With honest materials, axiom returned to 3/3 SA, meanflow moved to negative, and robomme was exposed as a medium-overlap borderline case.
