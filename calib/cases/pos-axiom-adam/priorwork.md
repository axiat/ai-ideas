Industry and Non-Paper Occupation: Searches of TRL issues #3218 and #2492, OpenRLHF, and verl found SGD only as a memory-saving configuration, not an RL-stage replacement study. OpenRLHF has long supported adam_offload, an engineering workaround that moves Adam state to CPU rather than removing components. OpenRLHF Muon support in v0.10.2 postdates the snapshot. The 2025-09 Thinking Machines blog LoRA Without Regret, now referenced in official TRL documentation, argues that each RL episode carries little information and rank-1 LoRA can match full-parameter updates; it concerns parameterization capacity rather than optimizer components.
Nearest Work:
- Why Transformers Need Adam: A Hessian Perspective | https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.16788 | 2024-02 | Explains SGD failure in transformers through heterogeneous block Hessians | Strongest argument that Adam is necessary, but only for next-token training and not RL.
- RL Finetunes Small Subnetworks in LLMs | https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.11711 | 2025-05, NeurIPS 2025 | Finds intrinsically sparse RL updates across 5-30% of parameters, 7 algorithms, and 10 models without sparse regularization | Direct empirical predecessor for the crack narrative; it observes sparsity without removing optimizer components.
- The Path Not Taken: RLVR Provably Learns Off the Principals | https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08567 | 2025-11 | Argues RLVR updates concentrate in low-curvature off-principal directions and that sparsity is only the surface symptom | Supports distinct RL geometry without testing optimizer choice.
- Kimi K2 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.20534 | 2025-07 | Technical report using Muon in production RL, stating "As in SFT, we employ the Muon optimizer" | Industrial evidence that RL need not use Adam, but it chooses a stronger optimizer rather than removing adaptive rates and momentum.
- Where Did My Optimum Go?(Henderson et al.) | https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.02525 | 2018-10 | Classic empirical study of optimizer choice for deep-RL policy gradients | Direct predecessor for RL optimizer choice outside LLM and RLVR; its results favor adaptive methods.
- The Marginal Value of Adaptive Gradient Methods | https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.08292 | 2017-05 | Finds adaptive methods generalize worse than tuned SGD on several tasks | Source of the challenge to universal Adam necessity, limited to small supervised settings.
- Full Parameter Fine-tuning with Limited Resources(LOMO) | https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.09782 | 2023-06 | Uses SGD-like fused updates for full-parameter fine-tuning of a 65B model | Evidence that post-training tolerates simpler optimizers; covers SFT, not RL.
- GaLore: Gradient Low-Rank Projection | https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.03507 | 2024-03 | Compresses optimizer state through low-rank gradient projection | Existing answer to the same forcing constraint by compression rather than removing adaptivity; evaluated in pretraining and SFT.
