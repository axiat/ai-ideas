## I1
One-Sentence Story: Add a provable deviation budget to trajectory-diffusion caching: sampler sensitivity maps each cached-step error to final trajectory displacement, while conformal calibration supplies distribution-free finite-sample coverage and turns empirical "lossless acceleration" into a testable contract.
Theme: Efficiency and Systems
Form: new mechanism or new problem
Summary: Iterative denoising keeps diffusion planners and policies outside real-time control budgets. Distillation routes, including consistency distillation and one-step generation, require retraining and remain coupled to the base model. Training-free cache routes reuse temporally redundant visual features or robot-policy activations through block scheduling and pruning, but their lossless claims are empirical. They do not quantify the distance between cached and full-compute rollouts. In closed-loop control, cached-step error propagates through the remaining sampler steps into trajectory displacement and possible safety violations rather than a perceptual-quality loss. The proposal scores reuse risk with inexpensive probe features, weights each step by an analytical sampler-sensitivity coefficient, and uses conformal calibration to guarantee that cached-rollout deviation from full-compute rollout is ≤η at a user-selected risk level. The deviation budget decides whether each step reuses or recomputes its activation.
Minimal Falsification Experiment: Use 1×H100 and two open-source trajectory-diffusion backbones, one Diffuser-style navigation model and one Diffusion Policy manipulation model. Implement probe scoring, sampler sensitivity, and conformal calibration. Nominal 90% and 95% coverage must hold on held-out rollouts. At the same 3-4× acceleration, budgeted decisions must match or outperform block-level heuristic caching on trajectory deviation and task success while supplying the coverage certificate. Kill the wager if sensitivity has near-zero correlation with measured displacement, making error propagation analytically useless, or if empirical coverage systematically misses its target. Budget: several days.
Why It May Be Novel: Diffusion acceleration is densely occupied, but existing cache methods are heuristic. Visual methods measure perceptual or pixel error, while robot methods call block-level caching lossless without a definition or guarantee. No work has been shown to model cached-step error propagation into closed-loop trajectory displacement, provide a distribution-free coverage guarantee for it, or use conformal calibration to drive diffusion-inference caching decisions. This remains a hypothesis for independent verification.
