## I1
One-Sentence Story: Remove torque-ground-truth supervision from actuator dynamics: use differentiable simulation to backpropagate pose-trajectory agreement into a simulator-equivalent torque surrogate, giving a $500-class servo arm both a dynamics model and sensorless force estimation.
Theme: Efficiency and Systems
Form: remove-load-bearing-assumption
Summary: Low-cost servo arms in the Dynamixel and Feetech ecosystems are becoming central embodied-data platforms, but their hardware tier lacks dynamics and force sensing. The linear calibration τ=Kt·I fails under friction, hysteresis, backlash, and thermal effects, while joint-torque and F/T sensors are unaffordable. The wager is that an actuator network taking motor telemetry, commands, position error, velocity, and current can learn a simulator-equivalent torque surrogate using only agreement between differentiable-simulation rollouts and measured pose trajectories. No torque ground truth is used. The same model supports sensorless external-force estimation at deployment; force labels for the auxiliary head come from a twin arm with a low-cost force sensor during teleoperation, while deployed platforms carry none. If successful, low-cost platforms gain both sim-to-real dynamics and force sensing for contact-rich tasks.
Assumption to Remove: Learning actuator dynamics requires torque-ground-truth supervision. ANYmal-style actuator networks use SEA spring deformation, industrial sensorless force estimation relies on calibrated current-to-torque mappings, and humanoid systems construct torque labels from accurate rigid-body models. Low-cost servos have none of these paths.
Why It Can Be Removed Now: Differentiable simulators such as MuJoCo MJX and Brax propagate gradients through articulated dynamics. Unified telemetry in the ALOHA and LeRobot ecosystems provides large pose-trajectory datasets. Torque-supervised actuator learning has established model capacity, leaving the supervision signal as the untested component.
Forcing Constraint: Cost and deployment. An SO-101 arm costs about $110 and OpenManipulator-X is in the $500 class, while a research-grade F/T sensor costs several times more than the arm and does not fit inside hobby servos. Current-to-torque calibration also fails at this hardware tier, reserving force control and contact-rich manipulation for expensive platforms.
Crack Evidence: https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.04217 — Differentiable simulation with neural augmentation can identify unmodeled contact friction and passive damping from trajectory observations alone, weakening the claim that every dynamic quantity needs direct measurement.
Crack Evidence: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.12685 — An ergoCub humanoid without joint-torque sensors uses a PINN to identify harmonic-drive friction from torque constructed indirectly through proprioception and a rigid-body model, weakening the requirement for direct torque measurement.
Minimal Falsification Experiment: Use SO-101 (~$110) or OpenManipulator-X with an MJX model. Collect several hours of randomly excited pose trajectories and train an actuator network from telemetry history to a torque surrogate using only differentiable trajectory-propagation loss. Held-out propagation error must beat both τ=Kt·I calibration and an ideal-actuator baseline, and the surrogate must recover the correct order of magnitude for endpoint force under known static payloads. Kill the wager if propagation error does not fall or payload force cannot be recovered because closed-loop firmware destroys torque identifiability. Budget: 1×H100 for several days and hardware under $500.
Why It May Be Novel: Existing actuator-learning work uses torque ground truth from SEA sensing, current calibration, or rigid-body reconstruction. Differentiable-identification work estimates global parameters or environmental residuals rather than per-actuator torque surrogates. Industrial current-residual and humanoid momentum observers assume reliable current-to-torque mappings or accurate rigid-body models. The only low-cost sensorless route is analytical DOB teleoperation, whose accuracy is capped by manually identified friction and hysteresis and which produces no dynamics model for simulation. No work has been shown to connect pose trajectories, differentiable simulation, torque surrogates, and force sensing on low-cost platforms. This remains a hypothesis for independent verification.
