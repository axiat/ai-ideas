## I1
One-Sentence Story: Reverse the assumption that sample-efficient off-policy RL needs a high update-to-data ratio: take only 2 gradient steps per 1024 new transitions, then recover convergence with a larger model, a larger batch, high-throughput simulation, and joint weight, feature, and gradient norm control.
Theme: Efficiency and Systems
Form: new mechanism or new problem
Summary: PPO remains the practical default for robot RL under massively parallel simulation because its on-policy updates are stable, although a narrow data distribution estimates value poorly in high-dimensional state-action spaces. Existing off-policy recipes split into two constrained paths. Fast methods use roughly 0.2M-parameter networks to optimize wall-clock time and cap asymptotic performance. Stable scaling methods regularize larger models but rely on many gradient steps per sample, or a high replay ratio, and remain slow. CrossQ established that UTD=1 with BatchNorm can match REDQ-level sample efficiency, but only with small networks and DMC-scale tasks; its later scaling work returned to higher UTD. The wager pushes UTD below 1 to the extreme regime of 2 gradient steps per 1024 new transitions while scaling model size, batch size, and simulator throughput. Joint control of weight, feature, and gradient norms must contain accumulated bootstrap error because weight normalization alone is insufficient. Success would move high-dimensional humanoid and dexterous-manipulation training from hours to minutes and make off-policy learning a viable default for sim-to-real.
Minimal Falsification Experiment: Use 1×H100 with IsaacLab or MJX on 2-3 high-dimensional tasks spanning humanoid locomotion and dexterous manipulation. Use a SAC backbone. The treatment is UTD 2/1024, a 4× model, a large batch, and joint weight, feature, and gradient norm constraints. Compare against the original small-model FastTD3/FastSAC recipe and SimbaV2 at high UTD under the same wall-clock budget. The low-UTD treatment must match or exceed both controls in final return without divergence. Kill the wager if extreme low UTD systematically diverges or materially trails the high-UTD control, showing that dense updates are necessary for off-policy convergence. Budget: several days.
Why It May Be Novel: The UTD literature concentrates on increasing or stabilizing high UTD through replay-ratio regularization, resets, and model augmentation. CrossQ is the only reverse-direction precedent, and it stops at UTD=1, small networks, no parallel-throughput study, and no robot sim-to-real result; its scaling follow-up increases UTD again. No work has been shown to combine sub-1 extreme UTD, model and throughput scaling, and robot sim-to-real. Fast precedents do not scale the model, while model-scaling precedents do not lower UTD. This remains a hypothesis for independent verification.
