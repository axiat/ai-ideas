Industry and Non-Paper Occupation: The official IsaacLab and rsl_rl stacks default to PPO, and repository configurations expose PPO as the practical sim-to-real standard. Raffin's 2025 report at araffin.github.io/post/sac-massive-sim documents failure modes for SAC under massive parallelism, including action-distribution and action-bound mismatch, and tunes the existing recipe rather than combining an extreme UTD reversal with model scaling.

Strongest Counterexample: CrossQ (1902.05605) occupies the mechanism axis by showing that UTD=1 plus normalization is viable. The remaining distinction is nearly three orders of magnitude in update density, from UTD=1 to 2/1024, plus small models versus scaled models, sample efficiency versus joint wall-clock and asymptotic goals, and the addition of parallel throughput and robot sim-to-real. Its scaling follow-up 2502.07523 moves toward higher UTD. On the payoff axis, the FastSAC/FastTD3 line, 2512.01996 and 2505.22642, already reaches 15-minute humanoid sim-to-real; its small networks cap asymptotic and high-dimensional dexterous performance, and stability comes from tuning and reward design rather than scalable norm control. Net-increment scoring must use both occupants as baselines and credit only asymptotic gains from scaled models at sub-1 UTD, dexterous high-dimensional gains, and the attributable joint-norm mechanism.
