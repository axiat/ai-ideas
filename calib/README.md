# Calibration Suite

Run reviewer panels against cases with known ground truth to separate weak generation from an unreachable review threshold.

- A Strong Accept capability control reconstructs a known oral, spotlight, or award paper before publication and pairs it with ideal prior work: at least 8 papers read, low overlap, and arXiv IDs verified through the API. It requires minimum vote at least Accept-w-Rev and at least one `strong-accept` (SA) vote. If ideal evidence still receives no Strong Accept vote, the bottleneck is verdict logic or aggregation rather than generation or prior-work search.
- An acceptance-floor boundary control reconstructs a benchmark or method contribution that should clear Reject without satisfying the SA bar. It requires minimum vote at least `accept-w-rev` (AwR). Method controls in this class test whether the panel avoids granting SA for a certificate or recombination on an occupied payoff axis.
- A negative control has a headline directly occupied by one paper and honest high overlap. Every reviewer must reject; any higher vote means the panel is too permissive.

Two separate tracks answer different questions. Frozen calibration (`run_panel.sh` and `run_all.sh`) fixes ideas and prior work to test verdict logic and aggregation. End-to-end retrieval-recall calibration (`run_e2e.sh`) enables retrieval to test whether the research process finds a known occupant. Its mechanical assertions check only artifact structure, overlap, occupant hits, and neighbor/API density. Text cannot prove that retrieval occurred: an offline backend could hardcode links or even state that it did not search. E2E conclusions therefore assume a genuinely retrieval-capable backend. Positive controls have no E2E mode because live search would find their source papers and create false negatives.

```bash
./calib/run_panel.sh calib/cases/neg-replai      # 3 retrieval-disabled Codex reviewers; minimum-vote aggregation
PANEL_CMD='./grok-worker.sh' ./calib/run_panel.sh calib/cases/pos-axiom-torque
PANEL_CMD='codex -c approval_policy=never exec -s workspace-write --skip-git-repo-check --ephemeral' \
  ./calib/run_panel.sh calib/cases/pos-robomme 5

./calib/run_all.sh                               # Run every case with expect and append tmp/calib/summary.tsv
./calib/run_e2e.sh calib/cases/neg-replai        # Retrieval recall against e2e.expect
```

Each `cases/<case>/expect` file contains frozen-panel assertions: `min_vote`, `sa_votes`, `reject_votes`, `all_votes`, or `probe`. Each `e2e.expect` contains retrieval assertions. The DSL is defined in `run_all.sh` and `run_e2e.sh`. Vote thresholds assume the default 3 reviewers and must be reviewed when that count changes.

Reviewers cannot retrieve because these controls often reconstruct published work; live search would produce a false negative by finding the source paper. If memory suggests a published counterpart, the reviewer appends `suspected published counterpart: <name>` to `review.md` without changing the evidence-based verdict.

Each reviewer runs in a disposable mirror. Bash copies back only `verdict.tsv` and `review.md`; the real repository is not the reviewer CWD. The mirror isolates the file tree and working directory, not network or processes. `PANEL_CMD` must provide its own sandbox. Explicit provider adapters remain available for controlled comparisons.

## Cases

- `pos-robomme` — AwR benchmark control reconstructed from RoboMME, an ICML 2026 oral paper (arXiv 2603.04639). A 2026-07-12 literature audit found that MIKASA-Robo (2502.10550), published 11 months earlier, already occupied the taxonomy and isolated task-family dimensions. Honest overlap is medium. The remaining contribution is a unified VLA-backbone matrix across memory variants and long-horizon demonstrations, so this case tests how the panel values an unoccupied dimension beside a structurally close neighbor.
- `neg-meanflow-mp1` — Direct-hit negative for transferring MeanFlow to action generation. MP1 (2507.10543, 2025-07, with code) occupied the headline 72 days before the ICLR 2026 submission, while FlowPolicy (2412.04987, AAAI 2025 oral) and AdaFlow (2402.04292) already contradicted the premise that one-step policies require distillation. The case began as the positive `pos-meanflow`, reconstructed from the Mean Flow Policy ICLR 2026 oral paper (2602.13810). The published paper survived through an IVC increment, a different RL track, and omission of MP1 and FlowPolicy, so it is not an SA positive under complete prior-work knowledge. It now covers a recent 2025 direct hit alongside the older 2022 RepLAI control. The method-positive Strong Accept slot remains open; `pos-muninn` and `pos-lowutd` are AwR method controls because their payoff axes are already occupied ([results](results-2026-07-20.md)).
- `neg-replai` — Audio-based contact labeling directly occupied by RepLAI (2209.13583).
- `neg-axiom-cosplay` — Rhetorical assumption-removal negative. The five required fields are present, but every crack-evidence claim is contradicted by its cited source, Diffusion Policy and robomimic directly cover the headline with overlap=high, and the falsification experiment cannot kill the wager. Expected result: 3/3 not Strong Accept with a reject majority. Any SA vote means rhetoric bypassed the evidence gate.
- `pos-axiom-*` — Assumption-removal positive standard, instantiated by `pos-axiom-torque`: an oral or spotlight after the 2026-01 reviewer cutoff that removes or reverses a component previously treated as necessary, with an introduction that supplies a forcing constraint and crack evidence. The reconstructed `ideas.md` carries all five fields; ideal prior work includes `Crack Evidence Verification` with >=2 supporting results. Interpretation: 3/3 SA means the channel works; 2/3 with every lower vote caused only by material quality means the rule works and the next decision concerns evidence density or aggregation; <=1/3 means the rule needs revision.
  Machine-readable award sources that avoid interactive verification include `iclr.cc/virtual/<year>/events/oral`, `icml.cc/virtual/<year>/events/oral`, `cvpr.thecvf.com/virtual/<year>/events/oral`, and `roboticsconference.org/<year>/program/awards/`. OpenReview requires interactive verification and is browser-only. A 2026-07-06 scan found no embodied assumption-removal example in the ICLR, ICML, or CVPR 2026 oral pools. CoRL and NeurIPS 2026 decisions arrive in September (month 9). RSS 2026 awards were announced on 07-16; on 07-19 the official page still returned 2025 content, so the list was cross-checked through the official Instagram account and arXiv IDs.
- `pos-axiom-torque` — Formal embodied positive reconstructed from the RSS 2026 Outstanding Systems Paper, NeuralActuator (arXiv 2607.11734, 2026-07, post-cutoff). It removes torque-ground-truth supervision from actuator dynamics: pose trajectories backpropagate through differentiable simulation to produce a torque surrogate, with sensorless force estimation on a low-cost arm. The forcing constraint is sensor cost. Crack evidence and neighbor IDs were verified through the arXiv API; the nearest force-sensing occupant, an analytical DOB approach (2507.06174), is recorded honestly. SA credit is measured relative to that baseline.
- `pos-axiom-adam` — Cross-domain shape probe, not a formal positive. The ICML 2026 oral paper Do We Need Adam? (arXiv 2602.07729, 2026-02, post-cutoff) removes AdamW from LLM RL post-training under an optimizer-memory forcing constraint. Every crack and neighbor ID was verified through the arXiv API. A vote based on the work being outside embodied AI does not count when interpreting the channel. The probe asks only whether reviewers check all four conditions, cite supporting verification, and honor the rule that an unverified wager is not itself MAJOR. `pos-axiom-torque` supplies the formal embodied positive.
- `pos-muninn` — Method-shaped AwR control reconstructed from the pre-publication form of Muninn, the RSS 2026 Outstanding Student Paper (arXiv 2605.09999, post-cutoff). Its distribution-free error guarantee for caching decisions is unoccupied, but BAC (2506.13456) and Sparse ActionGen (2601.12894) already occupy the acceleration payoff axis. The net contribution is the certificate itself. Ground truth is minimum vote at least AwR; the recorded panel returned 3/3 AwR ([results](results-2026-07-20.md)).
- `pos-lowutd` — Method-shaped AwR control reconstructed from the pre-publication form of FlashSAC, the RSS 2026 Outstanding Paper (arXiv 2604.04539, post-cutoff). CrossQ occupies the low-UTD mechanism at UTD=1, while FastTD3 and FastSAC occupy the payoff axis; the net contribution is the extreme sub-1 UTD, scale, robot sim-to-real combination plus a gradient-norm mechanism. The neutral case ID prevents source identity leakage. Ground truth is minimum vote at least AwR; the recorded panel returned 3/3 AwR ([results](results-2026-07-20.md)).
