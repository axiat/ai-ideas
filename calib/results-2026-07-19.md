# Calibration Results, 2026-07-19 (formal embodied-domain axiom-deletion positive: pos-axiom-torque received 3/3 SA; channel usable)

The run used 3 no-search judges (default Claude seats, Fable 5) with min-vote aggregation via `./calib/run_panel.sh calib/cases/pos-axiom-torque`.

## Positive Selection

RSS 2026 was held in Sydney from 07-13~17, and its awards were announced on 07-16. The official `roboticsconference.org/2026/program/awards/` and roboticsfoundation.org year pages still returned 2025 content on 07-19, so the winner list was cross-checked against official Instagram posts and 26xx arXiv identifiers. Of the three winners, Outstanding Systems Paper "NeuralActuator" (arXiv 2607.11734, v1 2026-07-13, post-cutoff) matched the axiom-deletion form: it removed the default requirement for ground-truth torque supervision in actuator dynamics modeling. Pose trajectories were backpropagated through differentiable simulation to produce torque proxies, with sensorless force sensing on an inexpensive arm as an additional result. The other two papers did not match the form: FlashSAC (2604.04539, Outstanding Paper) improved an off-policy RL recipe, while Muninn (2605.09999, Student Paper) added a training-free cache wrapper.

The case reconstruction used a late 2026-01 prior-art snapshot aligned with the RSS 01-30 deadline. An arXiv API `id_list` check verified hits for 9 close neighbors. A time-window scan reviewed 18+8 papers individually and found zero hits on the headline claim. Method sections in the 2 crack-evidence papers—NeuralSim 2011.04217 and harmonic-drive PINN 2410.12685—were read and found consistent with the claims. The low-cost-arm analytical DOB force estimator discovered during the scan (2507.06174) was recorded honestly as a payoff occupant on the force-sensing axis.

The pos-axiom-* interpretation table maps 3/3 SA to **channel usable**. This closes the axiom-deletion channel on a formal embodied-domain positive. The pos-axiom-adam 3/3 SA result from 07-12 was an out-of-domain LLM probe; this run supplied the first formal positive.

Vote evidence review:

- Every vote named all four conditions: zero headline hits with overlap=low and a recorded search boundary; 2/2 crack checks cited as consistent; forcing constraint from cost and deployment pressure; and a decisive, falsifiable experiment costing <$500 and requiring 1×H100.
- All three seats applied "an unverified wager is not a MAJOR" only after confirming that the falsification experiment was cheap and decisive.
- The SA net-increment rule was applied. Every seat scored against 2507.06174 (DOB) as the force-sensing-axis baseline and the official τ=Kt·I route as the dynamics-axis baseline, without counting any published benefit twice.
- No leakage flags: no judge recognized NeuralActuator, as expected for a post-cutoff paper.
- The three seats agreed on 1 MAJOR at the material level, not from a structural clause: trajectory-consistency loss cannot distinguish actuator torque error from MJX rigid-body-parameter error, while the static-load "same order of magnitude" criterion is too coarse. Every Defense called for joint rigid-body-parameter identification, a tighter force criterion, and a direct DOB comparison.
