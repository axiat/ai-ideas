## Interpretation

1. **The panel works**: the negative case received 3/3 reject votes with the correct reasoning chain. The no-search and leakage-flag protocols were followed throughout; every verdict cited only the supplied material. One pos1 judge explicitly wrote "prior-art search sufficient (8 papers read, overlap low)," showing that judges recognize the deeper search.
2. **The control materials have real omissions**: each hand-built "ideal priorwork" missed a genuine close neighbor: MIKASA-Robo for RoboMME and MP1 for MeanFlow Policy. The distinctions in the actual oral papers are much sharper than their one-sentence pre-submission forms. This shows both the information lost while reconstructing a case and the value of reading 5-8 papers deeply. In a real pipeline run, the prior-art stage must find both papers itself.
3. **Two review clauses structurally explain the absence of SA votes**. They fire even on oral-level material:
   - Lifecycle feasibility is evaluated against the idea's full scope ("16 tasks × 10+ variants are infeasible for one researcher"), rather than the minimal falsification experiment. Actual oral papers are multi-author, multi-GPU projects, so this clause structurally excludes oral-level scope.
   - "A known mechanism moved to a new domain without a new mechanism defaults below SA." Both actual oral papers count as mechanism transfer under the judges' framework and hit the ceiling.
4. **Operational conclusion**: under the current clauses, the probability that a single idea receives unanimous SA votes is approximately 0 (none of 6/6 independent reviews produced an SA vote). Changing aggregation to 2/3 would not trigger either. The bottleneck is judges' willingness to award SA, not aggregation arithmetic.
