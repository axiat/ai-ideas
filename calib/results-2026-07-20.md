# Calibration Results, 2026-07-20: two RSS 2026 method controls stop at AwR

The run used 3 retrieval-disabled judges in the default Claude seats with minimum-vote aggregation. Following the 07-19 `pos-axiom-torque` result, where an assumption-removal capability control received 3/3 SA, this run reconstructed two other RSS 2026 award papers as method and pure-new-mechanism boundary controls to test whether that form could reach SA.

## Cases

| case | source prototype | form | unoccupied headline axis | occupied axis recorded in the case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| pos-muninn | Muninn, 2605.09999, Outstanding Student Paper | pure new mechanism | distribution-free cache-deviation guarantee through sampler-sensitivity propagation and conformal coverage | acceleration payoff: BAC, 2506.13456, and Sparse ActionGen, 2601.12894 |
| pos-lowutd | FlashSAC, 2604.04539, Outstanding Paper | pure new mechanism | sub-1 extreme UTD × model and throughput scaling × robot sim-to-real | mechanism: CrossQ at UTD=1; payoff: minute-scale FastTD3/FastSAC humanoid training |

Both cases use the `pos-axiom-torque` evidence standard: a late 2026-01 prior-work snapshot, arXiv API `id_list` verification, individual review of a bounded time-window search, and full reading of each strongest counterexample.

## Results

| case | votes | min-vote | score |
|---|---|---|---|
| pos-muninn | AwR / AwR / AwR | accept-w-rev | pass |
| pos-lowutd | AwR / AwR / AwR | accept-w-rev | pass |

Both cases cleared the AwR acceptance floor and remained above reject without reaching SA. They calibrate the boundary between a viable award-paper proposal and a clear-accept result. The three judges independently converged on the same structure for each case:

- **pos-muninn:** The guarantee axis was accepted as genuinely unoccupied, with the Stronger dimension scored at 7-8, but the acceleration payoff was already occupied and the net increment was limited to the certificate. Two independent MAJOR findings remained: the case did not experimentally establish that heuristic caching actually fails, and it did not address exchangeability for conformal calibration under adaptive closed-loop decisions. As a pure-new-mechanism case, form 1, it does not receive the assumption-removal rule that prevents an unverified but cheaply falsifiable wager from counting as a MAJOR. At least 2 MAJOR findings therefore cap the verdict.
- **pos-lowutd:** The mechanism recombines CrossQ's low UTD, SimbaV2's norm-constrained model scaling, and one gradient-norm component, while FastTD3/FastSAC already occupy the payoff axis. The unverified net increment, asymptotic gains from a larger model and gains on high-dimensional dexterous tasks, did not reach clear accept. No dimension received an 8+ standout score, so novelty remained capped. The case is not a direct hit and its falsification experiment is decisive, which supports AwR rather than reject.

## Structural Finding

The observed SA path remains assumption removal. The 07-19 `pos-axiom-torque` assumption-removal control received 3/3 SA; both method positives received 3/3 AwR.

Assumption-removal cases have a break-glass rule: an unverified wager does not count as a MAJOR when the falsification experiment is cheap and decisive. More importantly, the complete `pos-axiom-torque` headline, low-cost servo pose-only torque surrogacy, was unoccupied. Muninn and FlashSAC place novelty on an occupied payoff axis: one adds a certificate, while the other recombines occupied mechanisms. The 07-19 net-increment rule correctly limits credit to gains attributable beyond the nearest occupants, and those increments do not reach clear accept.

The award value of both source papers comes from execution: a demonstrated 4.6× acceleration with real-robot validation for Muninn, and minute-scale humanoid training with component ablations for FlashSAC. At the idea stage, honest prior-work pricing leaves both at the borderline. This behavior is evidence that the standard does not award SA from award identity alone.

## `pos-lowutd` Setup Corrections

The first `pos-flashsac` run produced SA / AwR / SA and contained two setup defects. The corrected run produced 3/3 AwR.

- **Identity leakage:** The directory name `pos-flashsac` allowed a judge to infer the source paper. The neutral case ID is `pos-lowutd`.
- **Search gap:** One judge recovered CrossQ, 1902.05605, ICLR 2024, which already occupies the claim that low UTD plus normalization can converge. The first lexical query depended on the term `off-policy` and missed it. The corrected evidence includes CrossQ and its scaling follow-up, 2502.07523, and narrows the unoccupied axis from UTD reversal to sub-1 extreme UTD × scaling × the robot domain.
